What is Intrinsic Value?
In finance and investing, intrinsic value refers to the perceived or calculated value of a financial asset, such as a stock, based on its underlying fundamentals. It is the estimated value of an asset independent of its current market price. When it comes to stocks, intrinsic value represents the true worth of a company's shares based on factors such as its earnings, cash flows, assets, growth potential, and other relevant financial metrics. It is an attempt to determine the underlying value of a stock, taking into account its future earnings potential and the present value of those earnings.
